What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

My goal is to become a full stack developer and make nice looking websites while losing track of time changing 1 pixel to make it look aligned.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

A strength of mine is time management I’m able to learn effectively and reliably.

A limitation of mine would be trying to make my website perfect David has told us about the saying Don’t Let “Perfect” Be the Enemy of “Good” I had lost myself in the rabbit hole of trying to make everything perfect I should get all the work done first and then dive into it after.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would really like to develop my communications skills.

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives.

I manage the workload by setting a timeframe for when I study, I start at 9 and finish in the afternoon. I try to get the sprint done as quickly as possible so I can play games for the rest of week.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

When things build up, I’d take a break to play games or watch videos.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I will start researching the problem I’m stuck at and if I can’t find out the problem within 30 minutes I will message one of the facilitators to help

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitator to simplify difficult concepts making them easier to understand.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I value spending time with friends and family. I would set aside evenings and weekends for these activities, ensuring they don't interfere with my studies.
